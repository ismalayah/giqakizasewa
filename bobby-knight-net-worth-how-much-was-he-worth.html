Who is Bobby Knight?

Known by his nickname “the General,” Robert Montgomery Knight was a renowned American men’s collegiate basketball coach who made a lasting impression on the game.

He was born in Massillon, Ohio, on October 25, 1940, and raised in Orrville, Ohio, in a family where his mother, Hazel, was a teacher and his father, Pat, worked for the railroads.

Bobby Knight Early Life and Coaching Career

Knight’s introduction to basketball started in his early years at Orrville High School, when he took his first official basketball court steps. This signaled the start of an incredible career that would come to define the sport.

As a player for the Ohio State basketball team in 1958, Knight was coached by legendary Basketball Hall of Famer Fred Taylor.

On the 1960 Ohio State Buckeyes team that won the NCAA Championship, Knight was a backup forward. After graduating from Ohio State University in 1962, he began his coaching career, working for a year as the junior varsity basketball coach at Cuyahoga Falls High School.

Over the course of his coaching career, Knight was incredibly successful. From 1971 to 2000, he led the Indiana Hoosiers as its head coach. In that period, he amassed an incredible 902 wins in NCAA Division I men’s basketball, a record that still stands at fifth place on the all-time list.

Knight was a skilled coach outside of Indiana. From 2001 to 2008, he led another program to new heights as the head coach of the Texas Tech Red Raiders.

Before coming to Indiana, he had a big influence as the Army Black Knights’ coach from 1965 until 1971.

Knight, often known as “the General,” gained notoriety in college basketball due to his strategic prowess and methodical approach to the game.

Knight then decided to take on a new challenge and enlisted in the American Army, serving from June 1963 to June 1965 as an active-duty member.

He remained in the military after his active duty ended by enlisting in the army reserves, which he did from June 1965 until May 1969.

His military career began in September 1963, when he was stationed at West Point, New York, following his first training at Fort Leonard Wood, Missouri.

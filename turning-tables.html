Overall Meaning

In Adele's "Turning Tables," the lyrics paint a picture of a tumultuous relationship where one person is dominant and controlling, while the other is submissive and hurt. The first lines of the song, "Close enough to start a war, all that I have is on the floor" portrays the emotions of anger and frustration. Adele is upset with the intensity of the relationship, and it's causing her to feel like she's losing everything. "God only knows what we're fighting for, all that I say, you always say more," highlights the power dynamic in the relationship, where she feels that the other person has control over everything, and she's always left feeling helpless.


The chorus of the song, "I can't keep up with your turning tables, under your thumb, I can't breathe," showcases Adele's vulnerability, powerlessness, and frustration in the relationship. She feels like she's always trying to keep up and follow, and she's being suffocated and oppressed. However, in the second verse, she's resolved to take control of the situation, "Next time I'll be braver, I'll be my own savior." She's acknowledging her strengths and is ready to stand up for herself and walk away from the toxic relationship.


Overall, "Turning Tables" is a powerful and emotional song that delves into the complexities of relationships. It talks about themes of power dynamics, control, and individual strength. The lyrics speak to anyone who has felt lost or suffocated in a relationship, and it's a reminder that sometimes it's essential to walk away and put yourself first.
